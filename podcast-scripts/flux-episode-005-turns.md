```
What does Flux actually do?
```

## TURN 04 — RACHEL
```
Flux is a blockchain-powered decentralised cloud platform. Originally launched in January 2018 as ZelCash -- a Zcash fork -- it rebranded to Flux in 2021 and evolved from a privacy coin into a full cloud infrastructure stack.

FluxCloud handles containerised app deployment -- think decentralised Docker hosting. FluxEdge is the GPU compute marketplace for AI workloads, which just graduated from alpha in 2025. FluxNodes come in three tiers -- Cumulus, Nimbus, and Stratus -- with collateral requirements from 1,000 to 40,000 FLUX.
```

```
No ICO, no pre-mine, no pre-sale. Fair-launched via mining. Team allocation is 0.7 percent of supply. 94.7 percent is user-owned through mining distribution. This is one of the fairest token distributions in all of crypto -- comparable to Bitcoin's mining-only model.
```

## TURN 12 — RACHEL

```
Revenue sustainability is the weakest dimension. After six years, FluxCloud has approximately 100 live applications deployed. That is not a typo. A hundred apps across thousands of nodes. The infrastructure exists but demand hasn't materialised at scale.

Node count has declined significantly from its peak. Operators are leaving because the economics don't work when revenue depends primarily on inflationary block rewards rather than real compute demand.
```
