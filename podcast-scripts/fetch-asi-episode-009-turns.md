What does the alliance actually build?
```

Three layers. First, Fetch.ai's agent framework -- uAgents is a Python framework for building autonomous AI agents that register on-chain, discover each other, and transact in FET. It's the most mature open-source agent framework in the DeAI space, launched in 2022, Apache 2.0 licensed.

Second, SingularityNET's AI services marketplace -- publishing and consuming AI models across Ethereum and Cardano. It's been live since 2017.

Third, CUDOS contributed cloud compute and 30 validators. ASI Cloud exited beta in December 2025, claiming prices 50 percent cheaper than AWS. ASI-1 Mini is positioned as the first Web3-native large language model.

## TURN 07 — BOBSKI
```
The merger -- walk us through what happened.
```

## TURN 08 — RACHEL
```
Announced March 2024 under the ASI Alliance banner. Existing holders converted at fixed ratios -- about 0.43 FET per AGIX or OCEAN. CUDOS joined in October 2024. Ocean Protocol withdrew October 2025, alleging the governance didn't match the original decentralised vision and citing rushed decision-making.

Then it got ugly. Fetch.ai filed a lawsuit in November 2025, alleging Ocean's foundation had converted 661 million OCEAN tokens into 286 million FET and sold roughly 263 million FET on the open market without disclosure. That's over 10 percent of circulating supply. The lawsuit remains unresolved.
```

The Freedom Score lands in D grade territory. Key dimensions?

## TURN 10 — RACHEL

Infrastructure scores poorly. Fetchhub-4 runs on Cosmos SDK with CometBFT consensus, but it's capped at 70 validator slots. The top validators hold enormous stake. Core products like Agentverse, ASI Cloud, and ASI-1 Mini are all centrally hosted by Fetch.ai Inc.

Governance also scores poorly. On-chain voting exists, but Ocean Protocol's exit exposed the cracks. Each foundation retains independent governance, enabling strategic decisions without full community consent. The lawsuit is itself a governance failure.

The honest verdict?

## TURN 20 — RACHEL

The uAgents framework is genuinely good -- functional, well-documented, actively maintained. The team credentials are real. The Innovation Lab with Imperial College London is a serious academic partnership.

If SingularityNET departed, the alliance narrative collapses entirely.
