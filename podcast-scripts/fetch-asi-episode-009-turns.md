## TURN 07 — BOBSKI
```
The merger -- walk us through the full timeline, including how it ended.
```

## TURN 08 — RACHEL
```
Announced March 2024 under the ASI Alliance banner. Existing holders converted at fixed ratios -- about 0.43 FET per AGIX or OCEAN. CUDOS joined in October 2024. Then there was the Paal.ai proposal -- a plan to merge 900 million PAAL tokens into FET at a 6.24-to-1 conversion ratio with 180-day vesting. The community pushed back hard on the ratio and the vesting terms. PAAL dropped 70 percent on the news. The proposal was withdrawn in November 2024 before it even went to a vote.

Ocean Protocol withdrew in October 2025, alleging the governance didn't match the original decentralised vision and citing rushed decision-making. Fetch.ai filed a lawsuit in November 2025, alleging Ocean's foundation had converted 661 million OCEAN tokens into 286 million FET and sold roughly 263 million FET on the open market without disclosure. That's over 10 percent of circulating supply.

The lawsuit was settled in late 2025. Ocean agreed to return 286 million FET -- approximately 120 million dollars at the time -- and Fetch dropped all claims. GeoStaking, a validator node connected to Fetch.ai, mediated the talks. Sheikh agreed to cover all legal costs once the tokens were recovered.
```

## TURN 09 — BOBSKI
```
So the legal fight is over. Does that fix the governance problem?
```

## TURN 10 — RACHEL
```
It removes the legal overhang, but not the governance concerns. The settlement proved the alliance could recover tokens, but it didn't address why the situation happened in the first place. No post-mortem has been published. No governance reforms announced. Each foundation still retains independent governance, which is exactly what enabled Ocean to act unilaterally. The Paal.ai withdrawal showed the community can push back on bad proposals, which is a positive signal, but that's reactive, not structural.
```
