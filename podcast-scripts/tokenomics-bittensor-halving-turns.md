## TURN 05 — BOBSKI
```
Now here's what makes this different from a Bitcoin halving. This happened ten months after dee-TAO launched. How do those two things interact?
```

## TURN 06 — RACHEL
```
This is the part most analysis misses. Dee-TAO gave every subnet its own alpha token, traded against TAO via an on-chain AMM. When TAO emissions halved, the alpha tokens injected into subnet liquidity pools halved proportionally.

But -- and this is critical -- only the alpha tokens injected into subnet liquidity pools are subject to halving. Alpha rewards distributed directly to miners, validators and subnet owners remain constant. So you get a dual supply shock at the protocol layer -- less TAO minted -- and the application layer -- less alpha liquidity per subnet.
```

## TURN 07 — BOBSKI
```
So what happens to the subnets? Winners and losers?
```

## TURN 08 — RACHEL
```
The practical consequence is Darwinian. Reduced emissions starve weaker subnets of liquidity while productive subnets consolidate capital.

Chutes -- that's subnet 64, serverless inference -- is the poster child. A significant share of network emissions, a substantial market cap, processing billions of tokens daily. It's the dominant subnet by a wide margin.

Tiger Alpha -- subnet 107 -- is the cautionary tale. Launched with 200 TAO, peaked at 18 daily TAO emissions, then faced deregistration as capital fled. In a higher-emission environment, mediocre subnets could survive on the overflow. At half emissions, there's not enough to go around.
```

## TURN 09 — BOBSKI
```
There's also a first-mover problem here, right?
```

## TURN 10 — RACHEL
```
Yes, and it's structural. Subnets that launched before the halving accumulated liquidity at the higher pre-halving injection rate. They have a permanent advantage over subnets launched after December 2025. New subnets need to be substantially better to overcome the liquidity disadvantage.

This matters if you're evaluating which subnets to stake on. Pre-halving subnets with established liquidity are less fragile. Post-halving subnets face a harder path to viability.
```

```
About 7.3 million TAO staked -- 68 percent of circulating supply. But here's the interesting part. 72 percent of staked TAO is still in the Root network -- that's subnet zero. Only 28 percent is in individual subnets.

Dee-TAO was supposed to turn every TAO holder into a venture capitalist, allocating capital to subnets they believe in. Ten months later, nearly three-quarters of stake hasn't moved to the new system. The "every holder is a VC" vision hasn't materialised at scale.
```

```
Does the Bitcoin halving playbook apply here? Because everyone's making that comparison.
```

## TURN 14 — RACHEL
```
Bitcoin's history suggests the real price impact comes 12 to 18 months after the halving, not immediately. Bitcoin's first halving saw an 8,858 percent return peaking at 12 months. The second, 294 percent at 17 months. The third, 541 percent at 18 months. The fourth has been the weakest cycle, under 50 percent.

If TAO follows that pattern, the supply shock would materialise between Q4 2026 and mid-2027. The three-month decline is not inconsistent -- Bitcoin also declined in the weeks following its halvings.

But the comparison has real limits. Bitcoin is a monetary asset. TAO is infrastructure for AI workloads. And each Bitcoin halving matters less as the absolute supply reduction shrinks. TAO's first halving is its most impactful by definition.
```

```
What's the elephant in the room here?
```

## TURN 16 — RACHEL
```
Revenue. Or rather, the complete absence of it. Bit-tensor generates zero external revenue. The entire economic model runs on emissions. Chutes processes billions of tokens daily but that doesn't generate protocol revenue.

The more relevant question than "will the Bitcoin playbook work" is whether the halving forces subnets to develop real revenue models. If subnets start charging for inference, compute or data services -- creating actual revenue that supplements emissions -- that's the most bullish possible outcome. Whether they do it is the question.
```

## TURN 17 — BOBSKI
```
So what should TAO holders be watching over the next twelve months?
```

## TURN 18 — RACHEL
```
Five things. First, subnet revenue development -- does any subnet start generating real fees rather than just earning emissions?

Second, the alpha staking migration. If the Root-to-alpha ratio shifts from 72-28 toward 50-50, it signals the dee-TAO thesis is working. If it stays where it is, dee-TAO is a governance upgrade but not the economic transformation it was designed to be.

Third, subnet attrition rate. If 30 to 40 percent of the 129 subnets deregister, that's healthy -- resources concentrating in productive subnets. If 70 percent survive but with negligible activity, the zombie problem persists.

Fourth, the Grayscale ETF filing. If approved, it creates regulated passive exposure to TAO. The market clearly cares about this catalyst.

And fifth, the recycling effect. TAO spent on subnet registration recycles back to unissued supply, pushing the second halving further out. If network activity increases, the December 2029 estimate could shift materially.
```

## TURN 19 — BOBSKI

```
What's the honest take? Three months in, is this halving working?
```

## TURN 20 — RACHEL
```
The halving is real, verifiable and permanent. Supply dynamics are the cleanest in DeAI -- 21 million cap, no pre-mine, algorithmic scarcity.

But supply reduction alone doesn't create value. Bitcoin halvings work because Bitcoin has persistent growing demand from a global store-of-value narrative. TAO needs persistent growing demand from AI workloads flowing through subnets. That demand exists -- but it doesn't generate protocol revenue. The network's entire model is still a closed loop funded by inflation.

The dee-TAO interaction makes it more interesting than a simple supply squeeze. Reduced emissions are forcing natural selection -- concentrating capital in productive subnets, starving the rest. If that Darwinian pressure produces genuinely revenue-generating subnets, the halving will have been transformative. If it just means the same emission-dependent model runs at half speed, it's a non-event dressed in Bitcoin's scarcity narrative.
```
