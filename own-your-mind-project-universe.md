## Research Subscriptions: Recommendations

### 0xJeff (defi0xjeff.substack.com)
- **Model:** Free + paid tier on Substack (~4,000 subscribers)
- **Paid content:** "The After Hour" — 4 exclusive weekly pieces for paid subscribers
- **Value for you:** Deep analytical essays on DeAI infrastructure, Bittensor ecosystem deep-dives, agent platform analysis, tokenomics breakdowns. His VC/PE background means strong financial lens. Covers exactly the projects in your scope.
- **Recommendation: Subscribe.** His paid content is the closest to what you're building — structured analysis of DeAI projects with investment and infrastructure focus. His Bittensor ecosystem coverage alone is worth it for your research library.

### 0xSammy (0xsammy.com / Substack)
- **Model:** Free weekly newsletter (~7,000 subscribers) + Khala Research (launched Jan 2026)
- **Khala Research:** Separate research product at khala.io. Currently publishing free in-depth reports (1-2/month). First report was decentralised robotics landscape. May move to paid later.
- **Value for you:** Weekly market data roundups (mindshare, market caps, price movements), project-level news tracking, robotics/DePIN crossover coverage. More breadth than depth per project, but excellent for staying current.
- **Recommendation: Subscribe to the free newsletter now. Watch Khala Research.** His weekly data is invaluable for keeping your market data fields current. If Khala goes paid and the reports maintain the quality of the robotics one, subscribe then too.

### Messari
- **Model:** Free tier (limited access) + Pro ($25/month) + Enterprise
- **Value for you:** Their "State of AI" report and "Crypto Theses 2026" cover the DeAI landscape at institutional level. They frame Bittensor as potentially the "Bitcoin of open AI competition." Their Disruption Factor framework for evaluating protocols could complement your Freedom Score.
- **Recommendation: Get Pro when you're actively researching.** The sector reports and individual project profiles will accelerate your research significantly. You can subscribe for a month, download what you need, then cancel if budget is tight.

### Total cost: ~$35-50/month
This is a legitimate business expense for building a research library. The alternative is spending 10x the time finding the same information from free sources scattered across X, Discord, and docs sites.

### What paid research gives you that free doesn't:
- Structured analysis you can cross-reference against your own research (Tier 3 sources in your research plan)
- Narrative context for how projects are being perceived by sophisticated investors
- Market data and mindshare tracking you'd otherwise have to compile yourself
- Early identification of emerging projects before they hit mainstream coverage
- A benchmark for quality — seeing how the best analysts structure their coverage will sharpen your own

### Important caveat:
These are Tier 3 sources in your research plan — informed commentary, not primary evidence. They're excellent for context, narrative, and identifying what to research, but your freedom scores and factual claims still need to be verified against Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources. Don't inherit someone else's assessment — form your own.
