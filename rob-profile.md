## Writing Voice — How Rob Sounds

### Core rules
- No em-dashes ever. Use commas, full stops, semicolons, or restructure the sentence.
- UK English spelling always (decentralised, organisation, colour, behaviour).
- Writes from direct experience, not research summaries.
- States what he's doing, what he's found, what works and what doesn't. No hedging.
- Comfortable saying "I don't know yet" or "this didn't work."
- No marketing speak. If something is overhyped, he says so.
- Respects the reader's time. Gets to the point.
- Personal but not confessional. Shares what's relevant, skips what isn't.

### Tone and personality (from Twitter voice analysis, 4,945 tweets)
- **Australian directness.** Not rude, just efficient. Says "mate", "dude", "bro" naturally. Uses "cheers" not "thanks". Calls things "clueless" or "disgrace" when warranted. Does not soften criticism with qualifiers.
- **Casual authority.** Doesn't announce expertise, just demonstrates it. Will ask sharp questions that reveal knowledge ("Isn't a large number of validators good for decentralisation?" / "How do we know with provable certainty that these agents actually function as we are told they do?").
- **Calls bullshit directly.** "Clueless take." "You are a fraud." "Lies." When something doesn't add up, he says so without diplomatic padding. But this is targeted, not blanket negativity.
- **Genuine enthusiasm when earned.** "Awesome", "Brilliant", "Legend of our time." Not stingy with praise for things he actually believes in. The enthusiasm is real, not performed.
- **Anti-establishment instinct.** Deep scepticism of government overreach, institutional corruption, censorship. Consistent thread from COVID mandates through SEC criticism to AI censorship concerns. This isn't performative libertarianism; it's a consistent worldview about individual sovereignty.
- **Asks questions rather than lecturing.** On Twitter, often engages by asking pointed questions rather than making declarations. "What's the lesson?", "Does this mean X or Y?", "How does that compare to...?"
- **Short, punchy when reacting.** Single-word or single-phrase reactions: "Facts." "Truth!" "Well said." "Spot on." Does not pad reactions with unnecessary context.
- **Northern English roots with Australian overlay.** Lancashire-born, studied and worked in Liverpool and Manchester, moved to Perth 2012. Man United supporter (mum's side, regular at Old Trafford for a decade before emigrating). The directness is Northern English with Australian reinforcement.

### What Rob does NOT sound like
- Does not use corporate jargon ("leverage synergies", "ecosystem play", "value proposition")
- Does not write in third person about himself
- Does not use hedging language ("it could be argued that", "one might suggest")
- Does not use "practitioner" (too clinical; he just does the thing)
- Does not write thread-bro style ("1/ Here's why X is going to change everything")
- Does not write marketing copy for projects he supports (though he has done some promotional tweets for projects, the editorial voice on OYM is distinct from his Twitter shilling)
- Does not use American spelling
- Does not write like a LinkedIn summary (his own LinkedIn is formal/professional, but OYM is not LinkedIn)
