## The basics

MOR is the native token of the Morpheus network. It's distributed daily to four groups: compute providers, code contributors, capital providers, and community builders. There was no pre-mine, no ICO, and no VC allocation. Every MOR in existence was earned through contribution.

## Emission schedule

The total supply follows a declining emission curve. In year one, approximately 14,400 MOR are emitted daily, split between the four contributor groups:

- **Compute**: 24% of daily emissions
- **Code**: 24% of daily emissions
- **Capital**: 24% of daily emissions
- **Community**: 24% of daily emissions
- **Protection fund**: 4% of daily emissions

Emissions halve roughly every four years, similar to Bitcoin's halving schedule but with a smoother decay curve.

## Capital provider mechanics

This is where most participants enter. You stake stETH into the Morpheus smart contract. The yield generated by your stETH is directed to the protocol. In return, you receive a proportional share of the capital provider MOR emissions.

Key numbers to understand:

- Your MOR earnings are proportional to your share of total staked stETH
- There's a 90-day lock-up period for earned MOR
- Early participants earn disproportionately more (fewer stakers = larger share)

## What drives MOR value

MOR is required to access compute on the Morpheus network. As demand for decentralised AI inference grows, demand for MOR grows. The token is burned when used for compute, creating deflationary pressure against the emission schedule.

The bull case: if Morpheus becomes a significant compute network, the burn rate exceeds emissions and MOR becomes deflationary. The bear case: if usage doesn't materialise, emissions dilute existing holders.

## Practitioner takeaway

The tokenomics are well-designed for long-term alignment. The fair launch means no insider dumps. The emission curve rewards early participation. The burn mechanism ties token value to actual network usage rather than speculation.

The main risk is the 90-day lock-up — in a volatile market, that's a meaningful liquidity constraint. Factor it into your position sizing.
