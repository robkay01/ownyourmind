## Why this exists

I've been running decentralised AI infrastructure for six months now. Staking compute, testing protocols, breaking things, and learning what actually works versus what's just marketing. There's a gap in the coverage — most DeAI content is either protocol marketing or trader speculation. Almost nobody writes about it from the perspective of someone actually running the nodes.

That's what this journal is for. Weekly dispatches from someone with hardware on his desk and tokens in his wallet.

## What's on my desk

My primary setup right now:

- **Mac Studio M2 Ultra** (192GB) — Running inference for Morpheus compute
- **Intel NUC** — Akash provider node for lightweight workloads
- **Raspberry Pi cluster** (4x Pi 5) — Experimental, testing edge inference

Total power draw is around 200W at load. That's less than a gaming PC and it's earning yield 24/7.

## What I'm staking

Current positions as of this week:

- **MOR**: Capital provider with stETH stake. Earning daily MOR emissions. 90-day lock means I'm committed.
- **AKT**: Running a provider node on Akash. Modest earnings but learning the infrastructure.

Three things I'm paying attention to this week:
