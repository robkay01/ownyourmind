# DeAI Site Structure — Draft v1

## Editorial Position
**Freedom AND returns. Practitioner-led. Sovereignty-first. Hands-on.**

"The independent guide to participating in decentralised AI — why it matters for your freedom, why it makes financial sense, and how to actually do it."

---

## Primary Navigation (Top-Level Menu)

### 1. WHY DeAI
*The thesis. Why this matters beyond "number go up."*

- **The Case for Decentralisation** — Why centralised AI is a problem (surveillance, censorship, single points of failure, data exploitation). The libertarian case for sovereign AI.
- **Centralised AI vs Decentralised AI** — Honest comparison. What centralised does better today, where DeAI wins, and where it's heading.
- **The Sovereignty Stack** — Your data, your compute, your models, your inference. What full AI sovereignty actually looks like in practice.
- **Why Crypto + AI Converge** — Tokenomics as coordination mechanism. How financial incentives drive decentralisation (not just speculation).

> **Content type:** Evergreen essays. Updated periodically. This is your editorial foundation — the worldview that makes everything else coherent.

---

### 2. PROJECTS
*Independent, honest assessments of DeAI projects. Not shilling. Not "Top 10" listicles. Structured, practitioner-led reviews.*

**Categories:**
- **Compute & Infrastructure** — Morpheus, Akash, Render, Gensyn, io.net
- **Intelligence Networks** — Bittensor (TAO), SingularityNET/ASI Alliance
- **Agent Platforms** — Fetch.ai, Autonolas, Virtuals, ElizaOS
- **Data & Privacy** — Ocean Protocol, Sahara AI, Near Intents, Vana
- **Local & Personal AI** — Ollama, LM Studio, local runtimes
- **Emerging** — New projects as they launch

**Each project review follows a consistent framework:**
1. What it actually does (plain English, no marketing speak)
2. Value proposition — who is this for and why would they care?
3. Tokenomics breakdown — how the token works, emissions, staking, real economics
4. How to participate — staking, running nodes, building, or just using it
5. Honest assessment — what works today, what's hype, what's the risk
6. Freedom score — how decentralised is it really? (many "DeAI" projects are centralised with a token bolted on)

> **Content type:** In-depth project profiles. Living documents updated as projects evolve. This becomes the reference library people bookmark.

---

### 3. TOKENOMICS
*Dedicated section because token economics are the engine of DeAI and most people don't understand them.*

- **How DeAI Tokens Actually Work** — Stake-for-compute vs pay-per-use vs governance. The models explained.
- **Staking Economics** — Real yields, real costs, real risks. Not "APY" marketing.
- **Token Model Comparisons** — MOR vs TAO vs FET/ASI vs RENDER — side by side, same framework.
- **Emissions & Supply Schedules** — Halvings, inflation curves, what they mean for holders.
- **The Freedom + Returns Framework** — How to evaluate whether a token's incentive structure genuinely drives decentralisation or just enriches insiders.

> **Content type:** Analytical deep-dives. Some evergreen (the frameworks), some time-sensitive (specific token analysis). This is where your "returns" lens lives.

---

### 4. BUILD
*The hands-on, practical section. "How to actually do this."*

**Hardware & Setup:**
- Mac Studio / Mac Mini as a DeAI workstation — what you need, what it costs
- GPU considerations for local inference and compute contribution
- Running local models — Ollama, LM Studio setup guides
- Network and storage configuration for node operations

**Walkthroughs:**
- Setting up Agent Zero with Venice + Morpheus APIs
- Staking MOR and accessing decentralised compute
- Running a Morpheus compute node — step by step
- Setting up a Bittensor miner or validator (or evaluating whether you should)
- Building your first smart agent
- Contributing to DeAI projects as a non-developer

**What You Can Build:**
- Real examples of things built with DeAI tools
- Honest assessment of what's practical today vs what's coming
- Cost comparisons: DeAI tools vs centralised alternatives (Claude, GPT, etc.)

> **Content type:** Tutorials, guides, documentation. Heavily practical. Screenshots, terminal output, real configs. This is the section nobody else is creating.

---

### 5. JOURNAL
*The build-in-public stream. Raw, chronological, honest.*

- Weekly/fortnightly posts documenting what you're exploring
- "This week I tried X. Here's what happened."
- Experiments that worked. Experiments that failed.
- Discoveries, rabbit holes, connections between projects
- Your evolving thesis as you learn more

> **Content type:** Blog-style posts. Chronological. Personal voice. This is the most authentic section — the human behind the site. Think of 0xSammy and 0xJeff doing market intelligence; you're doing field reports from actually using these tools.

---

### 6. RESOURCES
*Reference material. The stuff people come back for.*

- Project directory — simple comparison table (name, category, token, status, links)
- Glossary — DeAI-specific terminology explained in plain language
- Recommended tools & software
- Community links — Discords, Telegrams, forums worth joining
- Recommended reading — best threads, papers, reports (including 0xSammy, 0xJeff, etc.)

> **Content type:** Reference pages. Maintained and updated. Low-effort to create but high-value for visitors.
